Method and formulas

Liljegren / OSHA: predicts both radiative natural wet-bulb and globe temperature from meteorological data using the Liljegren et al. heat-and-mass-transfer algorithm. It accounts for solar position, direct/diffuse radiation, atmospheric radiation, wind, humidity, and pressure.

Simplified heat balance: uses the psychrometric wet bulb plus a simplified black-globe energy balance with solar gain, surface albedo, longwave radiation, and convective cooling.

Outdoor direct-sun WBGT = 0.7 × natural wet bulb + 0.2 × globe temperature + 0.1 × dry bulb.

Shade/indoor WBGT = 0.7 × natural wet bulb + 0.3 × globe temperature.

A measured globe temperature overrides the simplified globe result. An entered official/measured WBGT overrides either estimated model for the controlling display.

Site estimates: all four Souda Bay profiles share the LGSA atmospheric baseline. The app then changes wind multiplier, albedo, and effective radiant load before recalculating each model.
